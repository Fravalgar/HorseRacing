Le trot

Harness trot races

Principle of harness racing :

  • Harness racing is a trotting race where the horse pulls a sulky and its driver behind it.
  • The main difficulty of this type of race is to go as fast as possible at the trot without ever going to the gallop. The risk is to be disqualified.

    • The greatest harnessed trotting races are :

  • Le Prix D'Amérique in France
  • The Habletonian Stakes in the USA
  • The Elitloppet in Sweden


    • Mounted trot races

    Principle of mounted trot races :

  • Mounted trotting races are trotting races where the horse carries a jockey on its back.
  • The main difficulty is the same as harness racing except that the jockey is on the horse, he is therefore more easily destabilized.

    • The greatest mounted trot races are :

  • The Prix de Cornulier in France
  • The Prix du Président de la République in France
  • The Prix de Normandie in France
